    Our Origins

    Starburst candy, originally named "Opal Fruits", was introduced in 1960 in the UK by the Mars Company. When the candy released in the United States in 1967, it was renamed to "Starburst". While the original four flavors remain iconic, starburst has been creating some fun flavors! Over the decades, Starburst introduced a variety of new flavors and spin-offs, such as tropical, sour, and even limited-edition blends. They even made fan favorites like "FaveREDs," which focus on the most popular red and pink flavors, and "All Pink" packs. Starburst Today Today, Starburst is a global phenomenon, enjoyed by candy lovers of all ages. Beyond the classic chews, the brand has expanded its product line to include gummies, jelly beans, and even drink mixes inspired by its signature flavors. Whether you're enjoying the original fruity squares or exploring one of the many modern variations, Starburst remains a symbol of bright, bold, and unapologetically juicy fun.
